Leather in the Stone Age

Here we begin to see some of the earliest tools dating back to Ancient Sumer around 5000 BC. Also the oldest know leather footwear was found in a cave in Armenia dating from around 3500 BC.

Leather in the Iron Age

Still used in clothing and footwear we start to see evidence of leather jewelry which might be considered a luxury during this time.

Leather in Ancient Greece

The popular leather sandal portrayed in movies this is where we begin to see leather bags.

Leather in the Middle Ages

Leather craft popularity continued to develop in the middle ages. Much more was done with tooling, painting, dying, and carving. Also was starting to get used for parchment books and boxes.

Leather in the Industrial Revolution

This time period saw a huge change in the leather industry. Machines allowed much faster production of finished leathers. The machines also used leather in the belts that drove them.

Leather Today

Today leather is used in high volumes across industries. Almost everyone has something made of leather. A wallet, saddle, or hat, its amazing to think how far leather has come over the years.
